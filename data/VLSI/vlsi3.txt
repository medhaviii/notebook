Short Channel Effects in VLSI

As transistor sizes shrink in modern VLSI technology, several physical effects appear that affect the performance of the transistor. These are known as short channel effects.

Channel Length Modulation:
Channel length modulation occurs when the effective channel length decreases due to an increase in drain voltage. This causes an increase in drain current even in saturation.

Hot Electron Effect:
The hot electron effect occurs when high electric fields accelerate electrons to very high energies. These energetic electrons can damage the gate oxide layer and degrade transistor performance.

Drain Induced Barrier Lowering (DIBL):
DIBL occurs when a high drain voltage reduces the potential barrier between the source and channel, allowing current to flow even when the transistor is supposed to be off.

Velocity Saturation:
At high electric fields, the velocity of charge carriers stops increasing linearly and reaches a saturation value.

Punch Through Effect:
Punch through occurs when the depletion regions of the source and drain meet, causing uncontrolled current flow through the device.

Subthreshold Conduction:
Even when the gate voltage is below the threshold voltage, a small leakage current may still flow through the transistor.

Short channel effects become more significant as transistor dimensions continue to shrink in modern semiconductor technologies.
