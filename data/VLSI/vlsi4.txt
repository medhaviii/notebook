CMOS Technology and Important Equations

CMOS stands for Complementary Metal Oxide Semiconductor. It is one of the most widely used technologies for designing integrated circuits in modern electronics. CMOS uses a combination of NMOS (n-type MOSFET) and PMOS (p-type MOSFET) transistors to implement logic functions.

In CMOS circuits, one transistor type conducts when the other is off. This complementary behavior results in very low static power consumption, making CMOS ideal for large scale digital systems.

Basic Structure of CMOS

A CMOS circuit generally consists of two networks:
1. Pull-up network made of PMOS transistors
2. Pull-down network made of NMOS transistors

The pull-up network connects the output to the power supply (Vdd), while the pull-down network connects the output to ground (GND). Only one network conducts at a time depending on the input signal.

CMOS Inverter

The CMOS inverter is the simplest CMOS logic circuit. It consists of one PMOS transistor connected to Vdd and one NMOS transistor connected to ground.

When the input voltage is low, the PMOS transistor turns on and the NMOS transistor turns off. This makes the output voltage high.

When the input voltage is high, the NMOS transistor turns on and the PMOS transistor turns off. This makes the output voltage low.

MOSFET Current Equation

The drain current of a MOS transistor operating in the saturation region can be expressed as:

Id = (1/2) × μ × Cox × (W/L) × (Vgs − Vth)^2

Where:
Id = Drain current
μ = Carrier mobility
Cox = Gate oxide capacitance per unit area
W = Channel width
L = Channel length
Vgs = Gate-to-source voltage
Vth = Threshold voltage

Dynamic Power Consumption

The dynamic power consumed by a CMOS circuit is given by the equation:

P = α × C × V² × f

Where:
P = Power consumption
α = Switching activity factor
C = Load capacitance
V = Supply voltage
f = Switching frequency

This equation shows that power consumption increases with supply voltage and operating frequency.

Static Power Consumption

In an ideal CMOS circuit, static power consumption is almost zero because only one transistor conducts at a time. However, in modern small-scale devices leakage currents cause small static power dissipation.

Propagation Delay

The propagation delay of a CMOS gate is approximately given by:

tp ≈ (C × V) / I

Where:
tp = Propagation delay
C = Load capacitance
V = Supply voltage
I = Current driving capability of the transistor

Applications of CMOS

CMOS technology is widely used in the design of microprocessors, memory devices, digital signal processors, and system-on-chip circuits.

Because of its low power consumption, high noise immunity, and scalability, CMOS has become the dominant technology in modern VLSI design.
