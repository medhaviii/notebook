Concept of Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, theft, or damage.

It ensures the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

Confidentiality:
Ensures that sensitive data is accessible only to authorized users through encryption and access control.

Integrity:
Ensures that data remains accurate and unaltered using mechanisms such as checksums and digital signatures.

Availability:
Ensures systems and data remain accessible through redundancy and disaster recovery planning.

Authentication:
Verifies the identity of users or systems using passwords, biometrics, or multi-factor authentication.

Cyber Attacks

A cyberattack exploits computer systems or networks using malicious code to steal or manipulate data.

Cyberattacks are classified into two types:

Web-Based Attacks

Injection Attacks:
Attackers insert malicious data into web applications to extract information.

Session Hijacking:
Attackers steal session cookies to gain access to user accounts.

Phishing:
Attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information.

Denial of Service (DoS):
Attackers overload servers with traffic to make them unavailable.

System-Based Attacks

Virus:
Self-replicating malicious programs that infect files and systems.

Worm:
Malware that spreads automatically through networks.

Trojan Horse:
Malicious programs disguised as legitimate applications.

Cyber Threat

A cyber threat is any potential malicious activity aimed at damaging systems or networks.

Threat vs Attack

A threat is a possible condition that could cause harm.
An attack is a deliberate action intended to cause harm.

Threats may be accidental or intentional, while attacks are always malicious.

Issues and Challenges of Cybersecurity

Cyber attacks from hackers, cybercriminals, and nation states.

Data breaches exposing sensitive information.

Security vulnerabilities in software and hardware.

Insider threats from employees or partners.

Lack of cybersecurity awareness among users.

Limited resources and expertise in organizations.

Ransomware attacks encrypting data and demanding payment.
