Internet

The word Internet comes from the term internetwork, which means connecting two or more computer networks.

The Internet began in the 1960s as a system used by government researchers to share information.

Early computers were large and immobile, and information sharing required physical transfer of magnetic tapes.

January 1, 1983 is considered the official birthday of the Internet when TCP/IP became the standard communication protocol.

TCP/IP allows computers on different networks to communicate with each other.

TCP establishes connections and ensures data packets are delivered in the correct sequence.

IP provides addressing and handles packet delivery.

World Wide Web (WWW)

The World Wide Web was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989.

The web is a collection of websites stored on web servers and accessed through the Internet.

Websites contain text, images, audio, and video content accessible worldwide.

Web pages are created using HTML and connected through hyperlinks accessed via HTTP.

Advent of the Internet

The development of the Internet started with research on packet switching in the 1960s.

ARPANET was the first interconnected computer network and was initially used by the military.

Later it was expanded to educational institutions.

Internet service providers emerged in the late 1980s and the Internet became commercialized in 1995.

The development of the Internet occurred in three phases:

Innovation Phase (1961–1974):
Development of packet switching, TCP/IP protocols, and client-server computing.

Institutionalization Phase (1975–1995):
Large institutions such as the U.S. Department of Defense and National Science Foundation funded Internet expansion.

Commercialization Phase (1995–Present):
Private companies expanded Internet services globally.

Internet Infrastructure for Data Transfer and Governance

Network Infrastructure includes backbone networks, data centers, and last-mile connectivity.

Protocols and standards such as IP, TLS, HTTP, and DNSSEC ensure secure communication.

Cloud services like AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud provide computing and storage resources.

Data governance includes data privacy regulations, access control, and encryption.

Content Delivery Networks (CDNs) optimize data delivery globally.

International collaboration helps regulate cross-border data transfer.

Internet Society (ISOC)

ISOC promotes Internet development and coordinates groups such as:

Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF)
Internet Architecture Board (IAB)
Internet Engineering Steering Group (IESG)

These organizations develop and review Internet standards.
